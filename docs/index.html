two ways to practice

exploration

a dyadic format. you speak what you're experiencing in the moment. aloud. asks brief, gentle questions to help you go further in.

mix and match attention focuses - body sensations, emotions, parts work - with vibes like playful, compassionate, or spacious. dial in how directive you want the guidance, from barely-there to actively steering.

noting

you pick the participants: AIs, fixed phrases, sound effects, or no one at all. each takes a turn naming a sensation in their awareness in one or two words.

experiencing the cycle of
rest → cue → notice → speak
turns out to be a surprisingly pleasant stabilizer of attention, and speaking helps keep you alert.
